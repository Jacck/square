Rectangle to Square Dissection Puzzle

Transform a 4×9 cm rectangle into a 6×6 cm square with one cut!
Step 1: Original 4×9 cm Rectangle

How It Works

Problem: Cut a 4 cm × 9 cm rectangle (area = 36 cm²) into exactly 2 pieces and rearrange them into a 6 cm × 6 cm square (area = 36 cm²).

The Cut: Make a stepped cut starting 6 cm from the left edge. Cut straight up 2 cm, turn left and cut across 2 cm, then turn up and cut 2 cm to the top edge.

The Pieces: This creates a larger L-shaped piece on the left and a smaller stepped piece on the right.

Assembly: Keep the L-shaped piece in position. Slide the stepped piece up and to the left so its protruding steps fit perfectly into the empty notches of the L-shaped piece, forming a perfect 6×6 square!
